Miami is a city known for its vibrancy and activity, and Saturday night at Kaseya Center, that’s what the men and women on the UFC 314 prelims brought with them to the Octagon.


 
From the opening bout through to the final featherweight pairing, each set of competitors stepped into the cage looking to entertain and impress, with each of the victors doing so in spades. Starting with Nora Cornolle’s submission win over Hailey Cowan and Marco Tulio lighting up Tresean Gore onto impressive finishes by Michal Oleksiejczuk and Julian Erosa, everything built to stand out efforts from Chase Hooper and Virna Jandiroba before wrapping with Dan Ige collecting a third-round stoppage win over Sean Woodson.
It was an outstanding early slate that sets the stage for what should be an action-packed main card, and we have all the details from the preliminary card contests collected for your review below.
The main event and co-main event are scheduled for five rounds. All other bouts are scheduled for three rounds.

In all three rounds, Jandiroba was able to pressure Yan back to the fence, change levels, and put her on the deck, dominating the interactions once the two were on the canvas. While Miller came out of the corner sharp, Hooper weathered the early storm and then went on the offensive, dominating the grappling entanglements against the tenured black belt. A decorated kickboxer, Cornolle brought the fight to the ground in the first, hunting for an armbar, only to end up stuck on bottom for the majority of the round, struggling to create space and get back to her feet.
