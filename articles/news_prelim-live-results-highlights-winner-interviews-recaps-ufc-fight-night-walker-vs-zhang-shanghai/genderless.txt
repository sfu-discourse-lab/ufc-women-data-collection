Back in Shanghai for the first time since November 2017, the action inside the Octagon gave the appreciative and vocal crowd at Shanghai Indoor Stadium plenty to cheer about and enjoy on the preliminary card slate.

And in the final bout of Saturday’s opening collection, Gauge Young and Maheshate went back and forth over 15 minutes, with the American coming out ahead on the scorecards and collecting his first UFC win.

It was a fun start to the day in Shanghai, and we have the details of what transpired collected for you below.

The taller Chinese fighter got the better of things in the opening round, leaning on his speed and length advantage to land the better shots throughout. Young found a rhythm in the second, mixing success on the feet with timely grappling to seemingly draw even before the two men ran close to level over the final five minutes, leaving the verdict in the judges’ hands.

All three judges scored the fight in favor of Rongzhu, who picked up his second win of the year. The 25-year-old continued to show improvements in his second stint with the promotion, and is an intriguing figure to track going forward in the 155-pound weight class.
