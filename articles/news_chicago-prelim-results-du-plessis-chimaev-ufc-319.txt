Saturday’s UFC 319 prelims at United Center offered a little bit of everything to whet your appetite for the pay-per-view main card that followed.
Opening with Joseph Morales winning the Ultimate Fighter 33 flyweight tournament with a second-round submission win, the remainder of the slate was a combination of competitive bouts that went the distance and impressive finishes, with Alexander Hernandez, Michal Oleksiejczuk and brand new Dana White’s Contender Series graduate Baisangur Susurkaev joining Morales in garnering stoppage wins.
It was a delicious assortment of contests to get the night started, and you can get yourself caught up on anything you missed by checking out the recaps below.
This page will be updated live throughout the night with full fight recaps and exclusive post-fight interviews with every winner.
Four days after earning his UFC contract, Baisangur Susurkaev earned his first UFC victory, claiming a second-round submission win over Eric Nolan.
One of two competitors to earn a contract Tuesday night on the first episode of Dana White’s Contender Series, the undefeated Russian lobbied to join his friend Khamzat Chimaev on Saturday’s card. Given the opportunity, Susurkaev had to endure a little first-round adversity against the game, natural welterweight Nolan before chopping him down with leg kicks early in the second. From there, he worked to the back, sunk in the hooks, and squeezed out the finish.
Now 10-0 as a professional, Susurkaev is unquestionably one to watch going forward in the middleweight division as he looks to make an immediate impact on the UFC roster. Don’t be surprised to see him back out there soon.

That’s now two camps with the Fighting Nerds and two first-round finishes for “Hussar,” who is still just 30 years old and has looked sharper than ever in 2025. Now 5-4 since moving to middleweight and 9-7 with one no contest in the UFC, the best may still be yet to come for Oleksiejczuk.
