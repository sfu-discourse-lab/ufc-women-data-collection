Saturday’s UFC 319 prelims at United Center offered a little bit of everything to whet your appetite for the pay-per-view main card that followed.


 
Opening with Joseph Morales winning the Ultimate Fighter 33 flyweight tournament with a second-round submission win, the remainder of the slate was a combination of competitive bouts that went the distance and impressive finishes, with Alexander Hernandez, Michal Oleksiejczuk and brand new Dana White’s Contender Series graduate Baisangur Susurkaev joining Morales in garnering stoppage wins.
It was a delicious assortment of contests to get the night started, and you can get yourself caught up on anything you missed by checking out the recaps below.

One of two competitors to earn a contract Tuesday night on the first episode of Dana White’s Contender Series, the undefeated Russian lobbied to join his friend Khamzat Chimaev on Saturday’s card. Given the opportunity, Susurkaev had to endure a little first-round adversity against the game, natural welterweight Nolan before chopping him down with leg kicks early in the second. Now 10-0 as a professional, Susurkaev is unquestionably one to watch going forward in the middleweight division as he looks to make an immediate impact on the UFC roster. Don’t be surprised to see him back out there soon.

The Polish middleweight fought with supreme confidence from the outset, marching down Meerschaert, looking to land with his hands. After tagging him throughout the frame, Oleksiejczuk landed a left that wobbled the Midwesterner and swarmed, dropping him soon after and pounding out the finish.

Drakkar Klose defeats Edson Barboza by unanimous decision (29-28, 29-28, 29-28)
Drakkar Klose collected one of the biggest wins of his lengthy UFC career on Saturday, out-working fellow veteran Edson Barboza in an entertaining and competitive three-round tussle.











                        Drakkar Klose punches Edson Barboza of Brazil in a lightweight fight during the UFC 319 event at the United Center on August 16, 2025 in Chicago, Illinois. (Photo by Ed Mulholland/Zuffa LLC)
              




Throughout the contest, the impact of Klose’s strikes stood out, both in the way they landed and the way they lumped up the Brazilian. As always, Barboza flashed his speed and diverse array of attacks, but Klose did well to deal with everything coming his way and land the more telling blows time and again.
All three judges scored the fight in favor of Klose, who advanced to 9-3 in the UFC with the victory and once again avoided suffering a second consecutive defeat. The MMA Lab product remains a fixture on the doorstep of the Top 15, and could garner an opportunity to face someone with a number next to their name next time out.

Barbosa was the more effective fighter in the first, dictating the terms of engagement both standing and on the canvas.
