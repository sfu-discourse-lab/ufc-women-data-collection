UFC 311 might be the best “opening pay-per-view of the year” in UFC history.

There has been a massive change atop the UFC 311 marquee, with lightweight champ Islam Makhachev now facing off with surging Brazilian finisher Renato Moicano to close out Saturday night's event at Intuit Dome. 
Makhachev might be the best pound-for-pound fighter on the planet today; a dominant force that has won 14 consecutive fights and is equally adept at beating opponents on the feet or on the ground. Seven of his last eight victories have come inside the distance, making the early suggestions that he was boring seem ridiculous in hindsight, and he’s only continued to elevate his levels since claiming the title, besting Alexander Volkanovski in back-to-back contests before submitting Dustin Poirier in his lone appearance of 2024.
 Moicano used 2024 as his breakout campaign, picking up three wins to extend his winning streak to four and his record to 6-0 in the lightweight division over his last six starts; his loss to Rafael Dos Anjos was a short-notice catchweight bout. Last time out, “Money Moicano” ventured across the Atlantic and wiped out Benoit Saint Denis in Paris, elevating himself into the Top 10 in the lightweight rankings as a result, and now, he's in the right place at the right time to challenge for championship gold.
Situations like this are always challenging, especially for the champion, as there is a great deal at stake in a dangerous matchup where Moicano enters with only upside. That being said, there may not be a fighter on the roster more capable of handling an 11th hour change of this nature than Makhachev, who has all the tools to dominate and the mettle to turn what could be a risky proposition into another iconic moment.
 Despite the change in combatants, the question remains the same: will it be “And Still” or “And New” to close out UFC 311?
