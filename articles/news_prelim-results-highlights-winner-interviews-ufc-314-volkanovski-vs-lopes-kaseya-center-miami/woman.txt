
In all three rounds, Jandiroba was able to pressure Yan back to the fence, change levels, and put her on the deck, dominating the interactions once the two were on the canvas. The Brazilian constantly chased submissions and positional advances, threatening with an armbar a couple different times, giving Yan few opportunities to get into her offense and have success.

Jandiroba is now, without question, the No. 1 contender in the 115-pound weight class, having earned back-to-back wins over former title challengers, and posted five straight wins over ranked opponents. We’ll see what’s next for champion Zhang Weili, but “Carcara” has put herself in a position to be the one standing opposite the Chinese superstar whenever she returns to defend her title.

A decorated kickboxer, Cornolle brought the fight to the ground in the first, hunting for an armbar, only to end up stuck on bottom for the majority of the round, struggling to create space and get back to her feet. In the second, she closed the distance quickly and ended up taking the back when Cowan was unable to rotate Cornolle fully with an uchi-mata, resulting in the French striker securing the position and collecting the choke.

That’s now three wins in four UFC starts for Cornolle, whose only loss came last fall against Jacqueline Cavalcanti. This was a strong effort, but one marred by Cornolle missing weight, the second time she’s done so thus far in the UFC.
