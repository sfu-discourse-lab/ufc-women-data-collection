The third annual Noche UFC event took place on Saturday evening at Frost Bank Center in San Antonio, Texas and there was a little bit of everything on display in the prelims.


 
Daniil Donchenko kicked things off by storming through Rodrigo Sezinando in the first round to win the Ultimate Fighter Season 33 welterweight tournament before Montse Rendon edged out 19-year-old Alice Pereira in her promotional debut. Flyweights Alden Coria and Jesus Aguilar earned quality wins on either side of a middleweight bout between Zachary Reese and Sedriques Dumas that ended in a no contest as a result of an accidental low blow.
The next two fights played out fairly similar to one another, with Tatiana Suarez surviving a late push to win her bout against fellow title challenger Amanda Lemos, while Joaquim Silva rallied to land on the happy side of the split decision verdict in his clash with Claudio Puelles. And in the final bout of the early slate, Dusko Todorovic wrapped things up by wrapping up a rear-naked choke finish over Jose Daniel Medina.
It was a hectic start to the evening that should serve as a lovely appetizer for tonight’s main card, and we have a recap of all the action collected for your review below.

The bruising middleweights were given the final place on the prelims because of their all-action approaches, and Todorovic made sure to validate the opportunity.  After busting up Medina early in the contest, the Serbian dragged his fellow DWCS graduate to the canvas, where he quickly took his back, sunk in the choke, and squeezed out the finish.




 

After opening his career with 10 consecutive victories, Saturday’s win was the first in four fights for Todorovic and only his fourth in 10 UFC appearances to date. It was a statement win in a critical moment, and hopefully can serve as a catalyst for continued success for “Thunder” going forward.




 
Joaquim Silva defeats Claudio Puelles by split decision (29-28, 28-29, 29-28)
Returning lightweights Joaquim Silva and Claudio Puelles clashed in a dogged back-and-forth in the penultimate preliminary card fight of the evening.











                         Joaquim Silva of Brazil reacts after defeating Claudio Puelles of Peru in a lightweight fight during the Noche UFC event at Frost Bank Center on September 13, 2025 in San Antonio, Texas. (Photo by Cooper Neill/Zuffa LLC)
              




Puelles wrestled well in the opening round and early part of the second, putting Silva on the canvas and controlling him, but failed to find submission openings or do much damage. Silva stung the Peruvian with a front kick to the midsection in the second and dove on a guillotine choke in the final seconds of the second. Puelles wrestled well for much of the round and staggered Silva at one point, only for the Brazilian to hurt him down the stretch as he tried to end things before the final horn.
The late rally was enough to earn Silva the victory on the scorecards, with two of the three officials awarding him the third round and the win. This was a gutsy comeback win for “Netto BJJ,” who picked up his first win since the close of 2023 while extending his record to 7-5 inside the Octagon.

Throughout the contest, Suarez initiated the action, closing the distance and forcing Lemos to grapple, controlling her along the fence for extended periods and depositing her to the canvas multiple times. Aguilar came out of the gates throwing everything he could muster at Gurule, looking to take him out with every shot while wading into the fray with his hands down quite frequently. Gurule had solid moments of his own, and the two ran fairly level in the second before Aguilar staggered the recent Contender Series grid in the third, with the two men trading quality shots right to the final horn.
When the tens and nines were tallied, it was Aguilar that came out ahead, registering his fourth win in six UFC appearances, while handing Gurule his second consecutive defeat. A member of the DWCS Class of ’22, Aguilar remains an entertaining, all-action member of the flyweight ranks and someone who should continue to get quality opportunities going forward.




 
Zachary Reese vs Sedriques Dumas ruled a no contest (accidental foul) at 0:51 of Round 1
The middleweight bout between Zachary Reese and Sedriques Dumas was waved off less than a minute into the contest after Dumas was rendered unable to continue following a thudding, accidental low blow.
Fighting in opposite stances, Reese fired off a left kick that hit Dumas below the waist with audible force, sending the Floridian to the canvas in understandable agony. Four minutes into his five-minute recovery period, Dumas said he was unable to continue and the bout was instantly waved off.
This fight had the potential to be a barnburner, but instead, it never really got started.

Montse Rendon defeats Alice Pereira by split decision (28-29, 29-28, 30-27)
Montse Rendon kicked off the night in successful fashion for Mexican athletes at Noche UFC, earning a unanimous decision win over newcomer Alice Pereira.
Competing for the first time in 18 months, the 36-year-old Rendon pressured throughout, looking to bring the fight to her debuting 19-year-old foe. After a pedestrian first round, Rendon made things grimy in the second, closing the frame on Pereira’s back, searching for a choke before the final frame returned to being a low-output, movement heavy affair until the final few moments.
