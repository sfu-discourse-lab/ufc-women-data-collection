Nate Landwehr is a fighter’s fighter. It doesn’t matter who, where or when. If “The Train” is healthy, the Clarksville, Tennessee, product is ready to scrap. That’s the attitude that prompted a five-fight stint in Russia and Kazakhstan where he scored five wins, three knockouts, the M-1 featherweight title, and, eventually, his ticket to the UFC. Since then, Landwehr made his mark the only way he knows how: loudly.
