Inside the stunning Intuit Dome, the UFC 311 prelims served as a delicious appetizer for the pay-per-view main event that is set to follow, with the action getting increasingly exciting as we progressed through the card.

An outstanding collection of fights on paper heading into the evening, the opening slate delivered, ratcheting up the anticipation for the main card and giving fans plenty to talk about to kick off the evening.

Here’s a look at what transpired.

Main event and co-main event scheduled for five rounds. All other bouts scheduled for three rounds.

The fight went the distance, with all three judges scoring the fight in favor of the Abdulmanap Nurmagomedov disciple. Ulanbekov has now won three straight and five of six inside the Octagon, cementing his place in the rankings and adding him to the growing list of streaking talents looking to make waves in the 125-pound ranks in 2025.

The experienced hand from Tajikistan stung Nakamura in both the first and second rounds, taking advantage of his tendency to keep his head on the center line by connecting with heavy overhand blows that put the unbeaten Japanese prospect on the deck. He remained on-point throughout the third, sitting Nakamura down almost immediately, backing him up with a side kick to the body, and generally dominating the striking exchanges.

While Nakamura had a brief period of control on the canvas in the third, this was an outstanding performance from Gafurov from the jump. After dropping his first two UFC appearances, the 28-year-old has now earned consecutive victories to establish himself as a dark horse in the talent-rich bantamweight division.

That’s now three straight wins and three straight finishes for the ascending light heavyweight from Uzbekistan. There were some dicey moments early, but Guskov dealt with it well and showed his class by cranking up the pressure and collecting the finish in the second.
