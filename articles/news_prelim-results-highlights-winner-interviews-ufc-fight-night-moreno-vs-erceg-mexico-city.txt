The UFC returned to Arena CDMX in Mexico City on Saturday night, and the action was as electric as ever.


 
After MarQuel Mederos and Austin Hubbard opened the show with a gruelling battle, Jamall Emmers registered a blistering knockout win over Gabriel Miranda before Loopy Godinez collected the first victory for a Mexican athlete on the night. Melquizael Costa earned his second win in a handful of weeks, scoring a unanimous decision victory over Christian Rodriguez in a competitive, back-and-forth battle at featherweight, with recent Dana White’s Contender Series graduate Ateba Gautier closing out the opening slate with a thunderous first-round knockout win over Jose Daniel Medina.
It was a great way to kick off what is sure to be a thrilling evening in the Mexican capital, and we have all the details from each fight collected for you below.

The opening round was competitive, with Rodriguez having greater success in the grappling exchanges, but Costa doing more damage in the striking realm. In the first round, Mederos did well, using his movement, speed, and low kicks to get the better of the exchanges and claiming an initial lead. But Hubbard worked to close the distance in the second, spending the majority of the frame pasted to Mederos’ back, searching for chokes, before putting the Dana White’s Contender Series grad on his back in the center of the Octagon early in the third. A tactical mistake allowed Mederos to get back to a control position and split Hubbard open with elbows to the side of the head, making things difficult to decide as the final horn sounded.
The judges were tasked with determining the victor, and when the scorecards were collected and tallied, it was Mederos that came out ahead, claiming the split decision victory. The change of positions and Mederos’ ability to land meaningful damage straight away surely swung the fight in his favor, and earned him his second victory in as many UFC starts to kick off the night’s action in Mexico City.
