While light heavyweight knockout artists top the bill in UFC’s return to Shanghai, the co-main event arguably bears the higher name value between arguably the greatest bantamweight champion in UFC history and a two-time title challenger who are both desperate to get some traction in the featherweight title picture.

Their respective skillsets should result in a highly technical chess match, and considering the high-stakes nature of the matchup, both in the division and to both of their career trajectories, there could be a slight sense of desperation for both men should the momentum start rolling in either direction.
