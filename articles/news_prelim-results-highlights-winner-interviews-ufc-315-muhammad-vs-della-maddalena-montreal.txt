A decade-long absence from Bell Centre ended on Saturday night as UFC 315 touched down in Montreal, with the action on the preliminary card delivering for the raucous masses right out of the gates.


 
Bekzat Almakhan kicked things off by ensuring the host nation didn’t go undefeated, knocking out Brad Katona with a beautiful uppercut. Following a competitive featherweight bout between Daniel Santos and JeongYeong Lee, French-Canadian Marc-Andre Barriault collected a first-round knockout win over Bruno Silva before tandem light heavyweight bouts went the distance.

In the final two bouts of the prelims, Canadians Jasmine Jasudavicius and Mike Malott went back-to-back, with the former garnering a first-round submission win over Jessica Andrade, while the latter defended home turf with a second-round knockout of American Charles Radtke.
It was an outstanding collection of bouts to kick off the evening, and we’ve got all the details from the prelims collected for you below.
Main event and co-main event are scheduled for five rounds. All other bouts are scheduled for three rounds.

UFC 315: Muhammad vs Della Maddalena Prelim Results



 
Mike Malott defeats Charles Radtke by KO (strikes) at 0:26 of Round 2
“Proper” Mike Malott wrapped up the prelims in Montreal with a return to his finishing ways, stopping Charles Radtke seconds into the second round.
Following a first round where the two men traded heavy shots and hard combinations throughout, Malott came out in the second and put Radtke on the deck with a clean left hook. The follow-up blows were pinpoint and punishing, putting Radtke out cold and sending the crowd into hysterics.

Erslan landed the better individual blows in the opening round, while the City Kickboxing man Stirling countered with superior volume and variety. The second was tightly contested with more periods of grappling and clinch work, before Stirling turned the second half of the round into a series of near-finishes as he unloaded on a spent and stunned Erslan.

Marc-Andre Barriault defeats Bruno Silva by KO (elbow) at 1:27 of Round 1
Welcome home, Marc-Andre Barriault!The Gatineau, Quebec native halted a three-fight slide with a first-round knockout win over Bruno Silva, finishing the Brazilian with a thudding elbow to the side of the head early in the opening round. You could see the emotion on Barriault’s face as he made the walk to the Octagon, and he used the support of the partisan crowd to propel him to a massive victory on home soil.

“Power Bar” has been a fixture in the middleweight division for the last several years, and though he entered with a ton of pressure on his shoulders, the 35-year-old turned it all into positive output on Saturday. Daniel Santos defeats JeongYeong Lee by unanimous decision (30-27, 30-27, 30-27)
Daniel Santos and JeongYeong Lee got into a fun back-and-forth in the second bout of the evening, trading blows for the full 15 minutes in an entertaining featherweight scrap.
Lee came out hot in the first, landing the better shots overall, stinging Santos with digs to the body and knocking him down at one point. But over the second and third rounds, the Brazilian did well to force grappling exchanges that limited Lee’s offensive output and forced him to be defensive, clipping his South Korean adversary with a few good shots between takedowns.
