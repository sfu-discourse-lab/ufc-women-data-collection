Back in Shanghai for the first time since November 2017, the action inside the Octagon gave the appreciative and vocal crowd at Shanghai Indoor Stadium plenty to cheer about and enjoy on the preliminary card slate.


 
Things kicked off with just the fifth Ezekiel choke in UFC history courtesy of Uran Satybaldiev, followed by a spirited three-round tussle between SuYoung You and Xiao Long. Three of the next four fights ended in spectacular fashion, as Yi Zha, Kyle Daukaus, and Charles Johnson all scored knockout wins, with Rongzhu and Austin Hubbard engaging in a quality lightweight battle mixed in there as well.
And in the final bout of Saturday’s opening collection, Gauge Young and Maheshate went back and forth over 15 minutes, with the American coming out ahead on the scorecards and collecting his first UFC win.
It was a fun start to the day in Shanghai, and we have the details of what transpired collected for you below.

Gauge Young defeats Maheshate by unanimous decision (30-27, 29-28, 29-28)
Gauge Young and Maheshate closed out the early slate at Shanghai Indoor Stadium with a quality back-and-forth in the lightweight division.
The taller Chinese fighter got the better of things in the opening round, leaning on his speed and length advantage to land the better shots throughout. Young found a rhythm in the second, mixing success on the feet with timely grappling to seemingly draw even before the two men ran close to level over the final five minutes, leaving the verdict in the judges’ hands.

Rongzhu defeats Austin Hubbard by unanimous decision (30-27, 29-28, 29-28)
Lightweights Rongzhu and Austin Hubbard engaged in an entertaining back-and-forth midway through the preliminary card slate, with the young Chinese veteran claiming his second straight UFC victory in the end.



 

Rongzhu leaned on his superior speed and diversity of attacks, chopping at the lead leg of Hubbard in the opening round and snapping home clean punches throughout. The resilient American did well to up his output and wrestle more in the second, but Rongzhu came out pressing in the third, taking the fight to Hubbard and landing the more meaningful shots throughout.

SuYoung You defeats Xiao Long by unanimous decision (29-28, 29-28, 29-28)
Road to UFC bantamweight tournament winner SuYoung You pushed his winning streak to five with a technical, sharp decision victory over local favorite Xiao Long early in Saturday’s fight card.
The South Korean was the more active and effective fighter through the first two rounds, landing at a much better clip while avoiding much of what was coming back his way. Long tried to rally as he did last time out, throwing nothing but elbows down the stretch, but You did his best to respond and work to the end of the bout.
