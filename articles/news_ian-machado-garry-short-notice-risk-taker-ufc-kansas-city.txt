Ian Machado Garry is the first to tell you how good he is and how good he will be.
Instead, he sees opportunity. He saw an opportunity to hand Rakhmonov his first loss, and although unsuccessful, he certainly earned the respect of many for taking the assignment. The same is true here. Prates and his fellow Fighting Nerds are on absolute fire, but it’s also a fight where Machado Garry could essentially take all that momentum for himself with a win. It’s a veteran move from the 27-year-old. He has the time to bounce back should he suffer a secondconsecutive loss, but a win over the 31-year-old Brazilian is the kind of victory that reignites his hopes for a title shot sooner rather than later. 
Whether someone loves or hates Machado Garry, two things are true: people care about what happens to him, and he absolutely loves everything there is to love about the fight game. If you have any doubts about his potential, just listen to one of his interviews: he’ll tell you how good he believes he is.
On April 26, he gets another chance to show everyone, too.
