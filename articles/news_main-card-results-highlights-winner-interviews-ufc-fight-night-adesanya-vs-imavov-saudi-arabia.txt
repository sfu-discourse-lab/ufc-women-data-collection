Following an electric collection of fights on the prelims, the higher stakes of the main card prompted the competitors to bring a more tactical and patient approach through to the main event, where Nassourdine Imavov closed things out with a statement win.
Lightweights Fares Ziam and Mike Davis kicked things off with a competitive and entertaining tussle, each man spilling crimson on the Octagon canvas before bantamweight prospect Vinicius Oliveira continued his ascent up the divisional ranks with a victory over Said Nurmagomedov. After Sergei Pavlovich and Michael “Venom” Page collected unanimous decision wins in their respective contests, Imavov wrapped things up by knocking out former two-time champion Israel Adesanya.

The 28-year-old Frenchman put the totality of his tool box on display in this one, working well on the feet, in the clinch, and on the canvas as well, mixing his weapons and showing a keen focus on creating damage when he worked to dominant positions. Davis had moments, especially in the scrambles and grappling entanglements, but Ziam did well to remain patient, work through any tricky spots, and continue delivering effective offence.
After splitting his first four appearances in the UFC, “Smile Killer” has found his stride as he’s matured as a fighter and a man. Unassuming outside of the cage, Ziam has developed into a dangerous all-around talent and someone to keep close tabs on as he continues to push forward in the 155-pound weight class.
These two sprinted out of the chute, with Nurmagomedov showcasing his dexterity and speed in the striking arts as Oliveira looked to bully him, close the distance, and land power shots. As the fight progressed and their gas tanks emptied, the Brazilian leaned more on his grappling, dominating the action in the final round as Nurmagomedov ran out of steam.
When the scores were added up, it was Oliveira that emerged on top, securing his third straight win to begin his UFC tenure and the biggest win of his career. “Lok Dog” continued to look like an intriguing new addition to the 135-pound weight class, and could find himself with a number next to his name when the rankings update next week.
Most anticipated the knockout artists would sling hammers and create a number of breath-taking exchanges, but instead, it was a primarily a chess match, with each man vary of the other’s power and stretches of each round playing out on the canvas. Each man landed their best singular blows in the third — Rozenstruik connecting with a left hand that checked Pavlovich’s chin, while the former interim title challenger countered with a stiff jab that backed “Bigi Boy” up — but each round ended with the Top 5 fighter grinding from top position.
This was an important victory for Pavlovich, who entered on a two-fight skid and needed to get things moving in the right direction again. There were flashes of the menacing power that carried him into title contention in the past, and it will be interesting to see if he’ll bring a little more urgency and aggression next time out, now that he’s back in the win column.
Page deployed his trademark “hands down” approach, strafing in at times to land clean blows while using his defensive movements to avoid taking any real meaningful shots in return. You could see as the fight progressed that Page’s timing and tactics were frustrating Magomedov and keeping him from finding any kind of rhythm with his striking.
This was an impressive win up a division for the 37-year-old Brit, who will likely head back down to welterweight for his next appearance. What is going to be more interesting is seeing how Magomedov responds to the first loss of his career, and who the UFC opts to pair him off with next.
There is a new contender in the middleweight division and his name is Nassourdine Imavov.
Following a measured and relatively even opening stanza, the emerging Russian pawed with a jab that captured Adesanya’s attention before coming over the top with a right hand that took the legs out from underneath the former two-time champion. While Adesanya tried to recover and defend himself, Imavov pounced and pounded out the finish.
Just an outstanding performance for the surging “Sniper,” who has now won four straight and injected himself into the thick of the title conversation in the 185-pound weight class. For Adesanya, it’s a third straight setback and second consecutive stoppage loss, leaving many to wonder what is next and what is left for “The Last Stylebender.”
But the night belonged to Imavov, who has now won four bouts in the last 12 months to cement his standing as a serious title threat in the 185-pound ranks.
