Inside the stunning Intuit Dome, the UFC 311 prelims served as a delicious appetizer for the pay-per-view main event that is set to follow, with the action getting increasingly exciting as we progressed through the card.


 
Opening with a series of decisions, including Grant Dawson registering the biggest win of his professional career, Bogdan Guskov and Azamat Bekoev turned in back-to-back finishes before Raoni Barcelos and Payton Talbott closed things out with an entertaining battle in the bantamweight division.
An outstanding collection of fights on paper heading into the evening, the opening slate delivered, ratcheting up the anticipation for the main card and giving fans plenty to talk about to kick off the evening.
Here’s a look at what transpired.
Main event and co-main event scheduled for five rounds. All other bouts scheduled for three rounds.

Ulanbekov leaned on his grappling throughout the contest, controlling large portions of the opening frame from top position, landing solid shots before finding a similar position, but less success in the back half of the second and third. Carpenter had his moments off his back in the first and on top in the second, but was never able to build enough consistent, significant momentum to truly threaten the Top 15 fixture.
The fight went the distance, with all three judges scoring the fight in favor of the Abdulmanap Nurmagomedov disciple. Ulanbekov has now won three straight and five of six inside the Octagon, cementing his place in the rankings and adding him to the growing list of streaking talents looking to make waves in the 125-pound ranks in 2025.

The experienced hand from Tajikistan stung Nakamura in both the first and second rounds, taking advantage of his tendency to keep his head on the center line by connecting with heavy overhand blows that put the unbeaten Japanese prospect on the deck. He remained on-point throughout the third, sitting Nakamura down almost immediately, backing him up with a side kick to the body, and generally dominating the striking exchanges.
While Nakamura had a brief period of control on the canvas in the third, this was an outstanding performance from Gafurov from the jump. After dropping his first two UFC appearances, the 28-year-old has now earned consecutive victories to establish himself as a dark horse in the talent-rich bantamweight division.




 
Ailin Perez defeats Karol Rosa by unanimous decision (29-28, 29-28, 30-27)
Ailin Perez and Karol Rosa continued the trend of fights going the full allotted time, pairing off for a low-output, tactical scrap in the bantamweight division to close out the UFC 311 early prelims.











                        Ailin Perez of Argentina throws a spinning back fist against Karol Rosa of Brazil in a bantamweight fight during the UFC 311 event at Intuit Dome on January 18, 2025 in Inglewood, California. (Photo by Jeff Bottari/Zuffa LLC)
              




Perez did well to utilize her wrestling at opportune times, changing levels and putting Rosa on the deck in both the first and second, neutralizing the pressure the Brazilian was beginning to apply each time. Rosa had more success in the third, keeping things standing and throwing with more force and intent, but never getting close to creating a finishing opportunity. 
When the scorecards were tallied, it was Perez that came out ahead, registering her fifth straight UFC victory. The charismatic Argentine is the owner of the longest active winning streak in the division, and should get another opportunity against a Top 10 opponent next time out after out-working the tenured Brazilian on Saturday night in Los Angeles.

“KGD” controlled the grappling entanglements throughout the bout, working back into effective positions in the spare moments when Ferreira attacked there and keeping the Brazilian pinned to the canvas whenever he initiated. Elekana surprised Guskov early, taking him down and climbing onto his back, locking in a body triangle and staying there, hunting for chokes, for four minutes. But Guskov got free and dominated the final stretch of the first, locking up a choke where Elekana was saved by the horn. He remained aggressive in the second and took the fight to the fading newcomer, punishing him with strikes before clamping onto a choke and drawing out the tap.
That’s now three straight wins and three straight finishes for the ascending light heavyweight from Uzbekistan. There were some dicey moments early, but Guskov dealt with it well and showed his class by cranking up the pressure and collecting the finish in the second.

The American Top Team representative and former LFA champion registered a first-round knockout win over Zachary Reese, finishing the DWCS graduate with heavy shots from inside his guard. The 38-year-old Brazilian wisely took Talbott down early and often, advancing positions and threatening with chokes, roughing him up with strikes when the time called for it.
