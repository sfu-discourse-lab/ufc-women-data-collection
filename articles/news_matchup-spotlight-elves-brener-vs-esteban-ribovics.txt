The lightweight division is known to have delivered some incredible fights over the years. That’ll be the case once again on Saturday night as two exciting prospects face off inside the APEX, as Brazil’s Elves Brener takes on Argentina’s Esteban Ribovics. The two have had very similar paths so far in the UFC, and both had their fair share of bangers inside the Octagon.
Both men will look to get things back on track after suffering losses in their last outing, and before they make the walk on Saturday night, let’s take a deeper dive into the matchup.
Elves Brener
If things play out on the feet, things could favor the Argentinean, who has a high 8.07 strikes landed per minute, compared to Brener’s 3.99. Half of Ribovics’ wins have ended by knockout, one of them being that insane knockout of McKinney last May.When it comes to takedowns and getting things to the mat, the numbers are interesting. Ribovics has a takedown accuracy of 66.7 percent, but Brener’s takedown defense is 76 percent, which could be another reason why this matchup stays standing.
The UFC average for strikes absorbed per minute is 2.5, but that is not the case for Ribovics and Brener, who both average around five strikes absorbed, which is another sign that this fight is one to tune in for and pay attention to, because anything could happen.
