
Nathaniel Wood kicked up the slate with a quality win over Morgan Charrière, dropping him in the first before cruising to a victory on the cards before Chris Duncan tapped out Jordan Vucenic in a clash between Scotland and England. Fan favorite Molly McCann was submitted by newcomer Alexia Thainara, announcing her retirement from the sport following the defeat, with Kevin Holland returning to the win column with a sharp effort opposite Gunnar Nelson midway through the six-fight run of action.

In the co-main event, Carlos Ulberg added to his winning streak with a unanimous decision victory over former champion Jan Blachowicz before Sean Brady dominated and finished Leon Edwards in the main event, putting himself in the title conversation in the welterweight division.

Sean Brady turned a short-notice opportunity into the biggest win of his career, venturing across the pond to defeat former welterweight champ Leon Edwards to close out Saturday night’s UFC return to London.

After a competitive first round where the two men engaged exclusively on the feet, Brady turned to his grappling and took the fight to Edwards, working to dominant positions, hunting for submissions, and landing quality strikes for the whole of the second and most of the third. As soon as the fourth round began, Brady put Edwards right back on the canvas, climbing to mount and sinking in a mounted, one-arm guillotine to secure the tap.

This was one-way traffic for the final two rounds and change, with Brady looking exceptional. He’s been tabbed as a contender throughout his UFC run and established himself as such here, running through the former champion, becoming the first to finish him.

For Edwards, it’s a bitter defeat on home soil and second straight setback, leaving with plenty to ponder as he heads back to the gym to figure out his next steps.

When the scorecards were totalled, all three officials saw the contest for the surging New Zealander, who moved another step closer to title contention with his eighth straight victory. This was the kind of measured, patient effort Ulberg continually needs to show as he progresses up the ranks, as it shows he has the skills and abilities to win competitive bouts against world class talents.

Holland put Nelson on the canvas with a long right hand in the final seconds of the opening round, dropping him with a similar shot to the one that finished Joaquin Buckley a few years ago. Nelson survived to the horn, but Holland dominated the exchanges the rest of the way, save for a third-round arm-triangle choke attempt that the Texan patiently worked free from.

This was a strong showing for Holland after getting quickly submitted by Reinier de Ridder at the start of the year. He’s much better suited to competing at welterweight, and it will be curious to see if he commits to fighting in the 170-pound ranks going forward or continues to division hop.

The Scotsman dropped Vucenic in the opening round with a clean right hand, spending the remainder of the frame controlling things with his grappling. Despite seemingly being the fresher of the two and having success on the feet, Vucenic drove forward into a takedown attempt and left his head outside, giving Duncan the opportunity to dive on his preferred submission and secure the finish.

That’s consecutive guillotine finishes for “The Problem,” who has won four of five since arriving in the UFC. He called out Terrance McKinney for a date in Miami, which might be too quick of a turnaround, but is a quality shout nonetheless.

Nathaniel Wood collected his second straight victory and maintained his extended run of success competing on British soil, earning a professional win over game Frenchman Morgan Charrière in the opening bout of Saturday’s main card in London.

Wood put “The Last Pirate” on the deck in the opening round, connecting with a beautiful right hand down the pipe, controlling and dictating things for the remainder of the round. Charriere had a little more success in the second, catching Wood with one or two that made the GB Top Team man offer a nod of recognition, but the Brit maintained his technical superiority and speed advantage, cruising to a well-earned win.

Wood has now won all four of his UFC appearances in his hometown and 10 straight overall in London, further cementing his standing as one of the top talents just outside the Top 15 in the featherweight division. He’s 5-1 since moving to the 145-pound ranks, and called for a step up in competition next time out following this one.
