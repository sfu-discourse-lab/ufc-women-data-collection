When the scorecards were collected and totalled, we had 30-27 scores in each direction, with Bukauskas landing on the happy side of the split decision verdict. That’s now two wins in 2025 and three straight overall for “The Baltic Gladiator,” who is 7-1 over his last eight outings.

Erslan landed the better individual blows in the opening round, while the City Kickboxing man Stirling countered with superior volume and variety. The second was tightly contested with more periods of grappling and clinch work, before Stirling turned the second half of the round into a series of near-finishes as he unloaded on a spent and stunned Erslan.

All three judges scored the fight the same, awarding Stirling his second straight decision win since joining the UFC roster. He’s still green, but you can see why there is excitement surrounding the unbeaten light heavyweight prospect from New Zealand.

“Power Bar” has been a fixture in the middleweight division for the last several years, and though he entered with a ton of pressure on his shoulders, the 35-year-old turned it all into positive output on Saturday. Just a momentum win for the French-Canadian veteran, who moved to 17-9 overall with his first stoppage victory in two years.

Lee came out hot in the first, landing the better shots overall, stinging Santos with digs to the body and knocking him down at one point. But over the second and third rounds, the Brazilian did well to force grappling exchanges that limited Lee’s offensive output and forced him to be defensive, clipping his South Korean adversary with a few good shots between takedowns.

The judges were tasked with determining the victor and all three saw it the same, awarding Santos the unanimous decision win. That’s now three straight for the Chute Boxe Diego Lima product, who took this fight on short notice and looked solid throughout despite being up a division.

After impressing in a short-notice loss to Umar Nurmagomedov last year, Almakhan showed out in his sophomore appearance in the Octagon here. Bantamweight is never short of talented fighters, but you have to add Almakhan’s name to the list of emerging standouts to track going forward after this one.
