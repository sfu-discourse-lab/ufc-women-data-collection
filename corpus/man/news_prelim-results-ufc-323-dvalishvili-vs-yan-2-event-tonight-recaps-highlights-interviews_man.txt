
Manuel Torres claimed the biggest win of his professional career to close out the UFC 323 prelims, sniping Grant Dawson and pounding out the finish midway through the first round.

Dawson’s initial shot attempt was forced and from too far out, proving to be a harbinger of things to come for the American Top Team man, as Torres simply got out of the way, forced the fight to remain on the feet, and made Dawson pay. With Dawson reaching for strikes at range, Torres slipped out of the way and landed a left uppercut that put him on the deck, quickly chasing him to the canvas and pounding out the finish.

“El Loco” is one of the most consistently entertaining fighters on the roster and this victory should propel him to even greater opportunities in 2026. The 30-year-old has now earned consecutive first-round wins and five such victories in six UFC appearances.

Chris Duncan survived the initial storm from Terrance McKinney, recovered, and collected a first-round submission win to extend his winning streak to four.

McKinney came out of the gates guns ablaze, hurting Duncan with multiple blows, but failed to find the one shot to put the Scotsman away. With the gas tank running low, Duncan worked back into the contest, dropping McKinney with an elbow along the fence before finally collecting the neck and the tap.

The 32-year-old has now won four straight, with each of them being entertaining in their own way, to establish himself as a mounting threat in the talent-rich 155-pound weight class. Problem by name, problem by nature indeed.

Fares Ziam ran his winning streak to six with a second-round stoppage win over Nazim Sadykhov to open the UFC 323 televised prelims.

“Smile Killer” fought to his strengths, staying long in the striking exchanges to accentuate his reach and using his wrestling to neutralize Sadykhov for extended periods in the first. After a largely uneventful second round, Ziam hurt Sadykhov with an elbow along the fence just before the 10-second clacker sounded, dropping him to canvas. From there, the French lightweight pounced, and when Sadykhov staggered while returning to his feet, referee Jason Herzog waved off the contest.

The 28-year-old is dialed in right now, fighting to his full potential and showing impressive maturation since his early days in the UFC. With great size for the division and growing confidence, we could very well see Ziam fighting for a place in the rankings next time out.

Brunno Ferreira earned the most meaningful victory of his UFC career on Saturday, out-hustling former title challenger Marvin Vettori over three rounds to collect his third straight win.

“The Hulk,” who came in three pounds north of the middleweight limit on Friday, was the more active and effective throughout, using a steady diet of counters and more powerful shots to get ahead on the scorecards. Vettori had a moment at the close of the first and late in the third, but wasn’t able to capitalize, allow Ferreira to gather his wits and continue exchanging through to the final horn.

A member of the Dana White’s Contender Series Class of ’22, the 33-year-old Brazilian is on the best run of his UFC career and advanced to 6-2 inside the Octagon. Ferreira is now 5-1 over his last six fights and inched closer to claiming a spot in the Top 15 with this victory over the tenured Italian middleweight.

Jalin Turner took a sabbatical from fighting earlier this and returned with a fury on Saturday, taking the fight to Edson Barboza from the outset and dispatching the Brazilian veteran in the first round.

“The Tarantula” cut Barboza with a lead elbow in the early moments of the contest and never allowed him to get into the fight. He stayed on the soon-to-be 40-year-old, but was measured with shots, taking his time to land impact blows that left Barboza staggering around the Octagon before Turner finally sent him to the canvas and secured the finish.

This was a brilliant effort from Turner, who is still just 30 years old. He has an abundance of talent and tremendous size for the division, where he was ranked in the Top 15 in the past. While a return to the rankings may not follow this victory, Turner is on the right track for earning a number next to his name again following his return to the win column at UFC 323.

What a chaotic way to kick of your UFC career if you’re Baraniewslki, who earned his place on the roster in September with a 20-second knockout win on the Contender Series. Now 7-0 as a professional and still brandishing a 100-percent finishing rate, the Polish judoka should be an interesting addition to the middleweight division if he continues to do things like this in 2026 and beyond.

Undefeated through the first 10 fights in his pro career, the University of Maryland product is a true blue-chip talent, having already amassed a 3-0-1 record inside the Octagon through his first 13 months on the roster. The Xtreme Couture representative is going to be heard from in the 185-pound weight class in the coming years, and it might not even take that long.
