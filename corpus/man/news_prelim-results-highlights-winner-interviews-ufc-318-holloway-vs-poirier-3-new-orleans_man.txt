
Louisiana native Carli Judice opened the show in style, finishing Nicolle Caliari in the third round before a run of five straight fights ended in the first round. Brunno Ferreira, Ryan Spann, and Jimmy Crute all collected submissions, while newcomers Islam Dulatov and Ateba Gautier dispatched their respective foes with strikes. The final three fights all went the distance, but they were all thoroughly entertaining and highly competitive, as Nikolay Veretennikov, local boy Brendan Allen and “Lok Dog” Vinicius Oliveira all got their hands raised.

Phillips started extremely well, clipping Oliveira in the opening minute of the bout, causing the Brazilian to look to wrestle. But once he cleared the cobwebs, Oliveira’s constant pressure and thudding power shifted the direction of the fight in his favor, as he returned the favor by hurting Phillips in the second and lined the better blows for much of the third.

“Lok Dog” scored two impressive wins in his rookie campaign last year and has now repeated that feat in his sophomore campaign. The Dana White’s Contender Series grad continues to inch forward in the 135-pound weight class and remains a captivating enigma on the fringes of the Top 10.

“All In” took the fight to Vettori out of the gate, busting him up early in the opening round. While the Italian veteran had positive moments in the back half of the frame and sporadically throughout the contest, it was Allen that consistently landed the more telling, impactful blows over the course of the three-round affair.

All three judges saw the fight in favor of Allen, who snapped a two-fight skid with the victory, simultaneously handing Vettori a three consecutive setback. Set to turn 30 in December, Allen has been a fixture in the rankings for the last couple years and likely earned his way back into the Top 10 with this victory on Saturday night.

Veretennikov was sharp and technical to start, hurting Prado when he slammed his way out of an armbar attempt before continuing to string together good strikes on the feet. But Prado survived and worked his way back into the fight, doing well when the duo hit the canvas and as Veretennikov’s gas tank started to wane.

When the scorecards were collected and tallied, it was Veretenniko that came out ahead, earning his first UFC victory in three appearances. This was an ultra-competitive battle and a classic example of why it’s so difficult to compete and thrive at this level.

Just 23 years old, Gautier has now earned consecutive first-round stoppage wins in each of his 2025 starts, pushing his winning streak to seven in the process. The Manchester Top Team product looks like a wrecking machine right now, and while the competition is going to get stiffer, “The Silent Assassin” looks like a future threat in the 185-pound ranks as he continues to gain experience and improve.

Just an outstanding debut effort from the talented, and streaking 26-year-old welterweight, who has now earned a dozen consecutive wins, all by stoppage. Dulatov looks like the genuine article, so be sure to keep tabs on his progress going forward.

Crute was a rising star in the 205-pound ranks earlier in his career, but an extended slide and an unhealthy obsession with the sport prompted him to press pause on his career a couple years back. Now, he’s back and he’s back in the win column after flashing the skills that still make him an intriguing figure in the division going forward.
