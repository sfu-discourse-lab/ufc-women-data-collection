
Things kicked off with just the fifth Ezekiel choke in UFC history courtesy of Uran Satybaldiev, followed by a spirited three-round tussle between SuYoung You and Xiao Long. Three of the next four fights ended in spectacular fashion, as Yi Zha, Kyle Daukaus, and Charles Johnson all scored knockout wins, with Rongzhu and Austin Hubbard engaging in a quality lightweight battle mixed in there as well.

When the tens and nines were totalled, it was Young that came out ahead, sweeping the scorecards to garner the first victory of his UFC career.

Kavanagh started well, taking the fight to Johnson throughout the first and showcasing more of his game in the early moments of the second as well. But as the prospect’s gas tank waned, Johnson cranked up the pressure and started finding great success, culminating with a clean right hand sending Kavanagh to the canvas in a heap in the late stages of the middle frame.

After having his winning streak snapped last time out, this was a massive return to the win column for the experienced and skilled Johnson. He owns a knockout win over No. 1 contender Joshua Van and will likely get a chance to fight forward in the rankings after this outstanding finish in Shanghai.

Lightweights Rongzhu and Austin Hubbard engaged in an entertaining back-and-forth midway through the preliminary card slate, with the young Chinese veteran claiming his second straight UFC victory in the end.

Rongzhu leaned on his superior speed and diversity of attacks, chopping at the lead leg of Hubbard in the opening round and snapping home clean punches throughout. The resilient American did well to up his output and wrestle more in the second, but Rongzhu came out pressing in the third, taking the fight to Hubbard and landing the more meaningful shots throughout.

After registering four wins on the regional circuit following his release, Daukaus kicked in the door in his return to the Octagon, picking up his first UFC victory in more than three-and-a-half years.

The first left hand Yi threw landed flush and backed Wilson up. As the broadcast team mentioned Wilson needing to get his guard up, Yi continued to fire rockets on that same side, connecting with force and leaving the lanky featherweight slumped along the fence.

A two-time Road to UFC tournament finalist and Season 2 tournament winner, this was a breakout performance for the 28-year-old Yi. After suffering a decision loss to Gabriel Santos in his promotional debut last time out, this was one of the most impressive rebound wins in recent memory and an emotional moment for the talented Chinese featherweight.

The South Korean was the more active and effective fighter through the first two rounds, landing at a much better clip while avoiding much of what was coming back his way. Long tried to rally as he did last time out, throwing nothing but elbows down the stretch, but You did his best to respond and work to the end of the bout.

Satybaldiev, who debuted at heavyweight on short notice and gave a good account of himself, returned to the 205-pound ranks and took it to Nurgozhay, dropping him along the fence early before starting to chase submissions. After losing a deep kimura, the former LFA champ transitioned into Nurgozhay’s guard and attacked with an Ezekiel choke, forcing the tap.

This was an outstanding performance for Satybaldiev, who was bounced from the ranks of the unbeaten last time out. The 31-year-old from Kyrgyzstan is an intriguing addition to the light heavyweight division, and is one to watch going forward.
