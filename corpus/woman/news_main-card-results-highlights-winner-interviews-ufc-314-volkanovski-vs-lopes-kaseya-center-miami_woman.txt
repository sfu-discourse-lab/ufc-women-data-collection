In the second, the Scouser again controlled the striking, but controlled things on the mat as well, before opening up the veteran with a flying knee early in the third that opened the door for him to continue attacking and ultimately pound out Chandler.

The former interim champion was the more active and effective throughout the first two rounds, using an array of kicks and quality movement to keep Pitbull outside and unable to find sustained success on the feet. In the third, Rodriguez was able to clip Pitbull with a right hand coming in that put him on the canvas, chasing him to the floor and putting a stamp on a statement win.

This was the most complete effort from the Chicago-based Rodriguez in some time, as he was locked in from the outset and continued having success throughout. He remains one of the top contenders in the featherweight division, and this victory puts him back on the short list of potential title challengers as things continue to shake out in the shifting 145-pound ranks.
