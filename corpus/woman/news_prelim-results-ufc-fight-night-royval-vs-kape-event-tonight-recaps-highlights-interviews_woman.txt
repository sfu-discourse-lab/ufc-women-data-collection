
The Squamish, British Columbia native clocked Bleda with a clean right hand straight out of the gates, doing well to stuff the takedown attempt that followed. Bleda remained dogged in her pursuit of the takedown, but Horth defended well, and as soon as they circled off the fence, the Canadian began attacking, rocking Bleda again and chasing down the finish.

That’s now consecutive wins for the 35-year-old flyweight, who registered her first finish since joining the UFC roster. Gritty and tough, Horth looked more fluid and confident with her hands here, and if she can carry that forward into next year, there will be an opportunity to add to her winning streak.
