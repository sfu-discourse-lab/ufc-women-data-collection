
Aldrich was her fundamentally sound self from the jump, constantly pumping out the jab and following it with clean lefts that regularly found the target and even sat Lee down at one point. The more dynamic Lee had some positive moments of her own, primarily with her kicking attacks, but couldn’t put together enough prolonged offense to keep Aldrich moving backwards and shift things in her favor.

For the second consecutive fight, the judges were tasked with rendering a verdict, and when the scores were tallied, it was Aldrich that came out ahead.

The victory gets the 32-year-old Denver native moving in the right direction again after dropping a decision to Veronica Hardy last year, and moves Aldrich to 3-1 over her last four outings. As for Lee, that’s now a record-setting six consecutive setbacks, all by decision, as she falls to 13-11 overall.
